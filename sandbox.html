Sandbox is an environment cultivated to support mini projects that align with The Language Project's mission of promoting multilingual education nationwide.


In technical fields, the term 'sandbox' refers to an incubator of sorts, a safe place to experiment and make mistakes. Thus, the Sandbox is an initiative that hopes to empower individuals passionate about language to spread this love through personal projects they conduct. It is of no doubt that among all the fields out there, technology is growing the quickest. With its fast growth comes an enormous range of applications. Thus, as an inherent part of The Language Project, the Sandbox intends to be at the intersection between language and tech. It is TLP's hope that in the long run, this union will help spread awareness on the power and significance of multilingual education.

Current Projects




The Multilingual Newspaper

Track: Language + Tech

This project aims to be a platform where intermediate learners of a language are able to practice while being immersed in a cultural context. News articles from the target country will be presented in a list view from which users are able to select one to read. While reading the article, if the user stumbles upon a word that they do not understand, they are able to click on it to be presented with the translation.


Pair-Up

Track: Language

This project aims to pair English as a Second Language (ESL) students up with foreign language learning (FLL) students in order to further immerse themselves in their respective studies of their language. A total of five one-hour sessions will be conducted in which pairs will connect through Zoom and have a conversation. The first half hour will be dedicated to one language, with the remaining time reserved for the other language. Upon completion of the five sessions, partners are free to choose whether they would like to stay with the same pair or join another round of matching.
